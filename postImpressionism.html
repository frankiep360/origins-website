Post-Impressionism

Vincent Van Gogh

Vincent van Gogh was a Dutch Post-Impressionist painter known for his emotional depth, bold colors, and expressive brushwork. Despite struggling with mental illness and poverty, he created over 2,000 artworks, including Starry Night and Sunflowers, which captured the beauty and turmoil of his life.

Van Gogh's innovative use of color and form expanded Post-Impressionism, influencing modern movements like Expressionism. His ability to convey raw emotion and spiritual intensity redefined artistic expression. Although underappreciated during his lifetime, he became one of the most influential and celebrated artists in history after his death.

Edvard Munch

Edvard Munch was a Norwegian painter and printmaker whose emotionally charged works explored themes of love, anxiety, and mortality. His iconic painting The Scream is a symbol of existential angst, characterized by vivid colors and bold, swirling forms that reflect his inner turmoil and personal struggles.

Munch's work bridged Post-Impressionism and Symbolism, emphasizing emotional expression over realistic representation. His innovative style influenced modern art movements, including Expressionism, inspiring artists to delve into psychological and emotional depths. Munch’s legacy lies in his ability to convey universal human experiences with raw intensity and pioneering visual techniques.
