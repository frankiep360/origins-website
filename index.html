Post-Impressionism

1886-1905

Post-Impressionism emerged in the late 19th century, focusing on emotional expression, symbolic content, and structural composition. It moved beyond Impressionism's emphasis on light and color, exploring bold, vibrant tones and distinct forms. This movement laid the groundwork for modern art by prioritizing individual creativity and deeper emotional resonance.

Surrealism

1924-1966

Surrealism, emerging in the early 20th century, sought to unlock the unconscious mind through dreamlike imagery and unexpected juxtapositions. Influenced by psychoanalysis, it challenged reality and traditional norms, emphasizing imagination, symbolism, and emotional depth to explore themes of desire, fear, and the irrational.

Pop Art

1950s-1980s

Pop Art, emerging in the mid-20th century, celebrated popular culture by incorporating imagery from advertising, comics, and mass media. It challenged traditional art forms, blending commercial and fine art through bold colors, repetition, and irony. The movement reflected consumerism and modern life, redefining art's relationship with everyday culture.
